\begin{problem}
Let $M$ and $N$ be modules over a ring $R$. Show that the set of homomorphisms $\phi : M \to N$ can itself be given the structure of an $R$-module, called $\Hom_R(M,N)$, and describe the following modules:
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
  \item $\Hom_\Z(\Z^m,\Z^n)$;
  \item $\Hom_\Z(\Z^m,\Z/2)$;
  \item $\Hom_\Z(\Z/2,\Z)$;
  \item $\Hom_\Z(\Z/2,\Z/3)$;
  \item $\Hom_\Z(\Z/4,\Z/6)$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{problem}
